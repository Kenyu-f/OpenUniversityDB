# Caltech — Graduate Research

`universities/caltech/graduate_research.md`

> Graduate education at Caltech is organized around ~30+ "options" (departmental programs) nested within the six divisions. Processes below reflect institute-wide Graduate Studies Office (GSO) policy plus representative division/option-level practices (Chemistry and Chemical Engineering; Physics, Math and Astronomy; Geological and Planetary Sciences). **Requirements vary by option — always confirm specifics with the student's own option/department**, since candidacy procedures, exam formats, and committee composition rules differ.

---

## 1. PhD Programs

- Caltech's PhD is organized by **option** (e.g., Physics, Chemistry, Chemical Engineering, Biology, Applied Physics, Planetary Science, etc.), each with its own faculty-run admissions and curriculum committee, coordinated centrally by the **Graduate Studies Office (GSO)**.
- The **average time to complete a Caltech PhD is 5–6 years**.
- Structure generally follows: coursework/qualifying requirements (Years 1–2) → advisor selection and candidacy (by Year 3–4) → dissertation research and thesis defense (Years 4–6).
- Caltech does **not require a minor** for the PhD at the institute level, though individual options may impose their own minor or breadth requirements.

## 2. Master's Programs

- Caltech offers **terminal Master's (M.S.)** programs in some options, generally with limited institutional financial support — the Institute states it does **not generally provide financial aid to master's-level students**, though M.S. students may be eligible for teaching or research assistantships where available.
- M.S. applications must be submitted **no later than "Add Day" of the spring term** for a June graduation (a general Registrar-driven deadline; confirm per-option specifics).
- To be awarded the M.S., a student logs into Caltech's **REGIS** system, selects "M.S. Candidacy," identifies the option and target year, and follows the option's plan-of-study and Institute requirements.
- Because master's funding is limited, a small number of terminal master's students are admitted **without financial aid** each year — Caltech is explicit that this carries "great financial responsibility" for the student, and outside part-time employment is **not permitted** for any Caltech graduate student (full-time study, including the summer term, is expected).

## 3. Advisor Selection and Laboratory Matching

- Caltech's PhD admissions and early graduate experience place significant emphasis on **matching a student to a specific faculty advisor's active research program** — more so than at some peer institutions with formal multi-lab rotation systems.
- Some options (e.g., Chemistry and Chemical Engineering) provide explicit guidance on **selecting an advisor**, including informal "rotation" conversations with multiple potential faculty mentors before formally committing.
- The **thesis advisor must be a Caltech professorial faculty member.**
- Advisor selection is typically finalized during the first year, and is a prerequisite for continued funding and for admission to candidacy.
- The Caltech Counseling Center provides guidance/resources specifically for students navigating advisor selection.

## 4. Qualifying and Candidacy Examinations

Caltech's terminology and process vary by option, but generally converge on:

| Stage | Description |
|---|---|
| **Basic/qualifying requirement** | Some options require a foundational qualifying exam (e.g., Physics requires completion of a "basic physics requirement" via written candidacy exams) before advancing. |
| **Candidacy examination** | An oral (and sometimes written) exam in which the student presents research progress and/or research proposals ("propositions") to a faculty committee. Common structure: **one in-field proposition + one out-of-field proposition** (e.g., in Chemistry/Chemical Engineering), designed to test both depth in the student's research area and breadth/creativity outside it. |
| **Candidacy committee** | Typically **3–5 faculty members**, the majority from the student's own option/division; the committee chair must be Caltech professorial faculty and **cannot be the student's own advisor**. |
| **Timing** | Varies by option — e.g., Geological and Planetary Sciences candidacy exams are normally taken early in the first term of the **second year**; Chemical Biology/Molecular Biosciences tracks expect candidacy before/during spring of the **second year (G2)**. Institute-wide, **PhD candidacy must be completed before beginning the fourth year of residence.** |
| **After candidacy** | Students typically meet annually with a **Thesis Advisory Committee (TAC)**, usually derived from the candidacy committee, to review research progress. |

Some options (e.g., Astronomy) additionally require candidacy committees with specific role-based composition — e.g., a theorist, an observer, an experimentalist, and a faculty member entirely outside the thesis field — to ensure broad, rigorous review.

**Consequence of non-candidacy:** A student not admitted to candidacy by a specified point (institute-wide: before Year 4; some options specify by end of Year 3) generally needs special permission from the division/option to continue registration.

## 5. Dissertation ("Thesis") Process

- Caltech uses the term **"thesis"** for the PhD dissertation. All PhD programs require the student to **prepare, submit, and orally defend** a written thesis.
- Formatting follows the Graduate Office's **"Regulations for the Format and Presentation of a Graduate Thesis."**
- **Final Examination (Defense) Committee:** a minimum of **4 members**, at least 3 of whom must be current Caltech professorial faculty; the chair must be Caltech professorial faculty and distinct from the advisor.
- Upon successful defense, students must upload the final, approved thesis to **CaltechTHESIS**, the Institute's thesis repository (see `publications.md`), and complete Registrar checkout procedures.
- The Graduate Office offers proofreading support (minimum 3 weeks' lead time, with a Proofreader Agreement form required).
- Annual progress reviews with the option/division and thesis committee are standard; a student judged to be making inadequate progress may, per option-level faculty recommendation, be denied permission to continue in the PhD program.
- After the fifth academic year, continued registration in some options (e.g., GPS) requires a formal annual petition; financial aid is not normally extended beyond the sixth year.

## 6. Teaching Assistantships (TA / "GTA")

- Nearly all Caltech PhD students hold at least one **Graduate Teaching Assistant (GTA)** appointment during their studies; in some divisions (e.g., Physics, Math, Astronomy) a TA is the **predominant mode of funding** after the first year.
- Example requirement: Chemistry and Chemical Engineering PhD students must complete a **minimum of 24 term-hours of GTA duties**, typically assigned after the first year in residence.
- Physics/Math/Astronomy TA loads are described as "very light," typically assisting with an introductory (freshman/sophomore-level) course.
- Where a program requires teaching experience but the student is already funded at or above the minimum stipend by another source, a **mentored (unpaid) teaching experience** may substitute for a formal GTA appointment.

## 7. Research Assistantships (RA / "GRA")

- Graduate Research Assistant appointments involve conducting mentored research (including grant-application support, protocol/study design, conference presentation, and maintaining research databases/equipment) under a faculty PI.
- RA appointments are contingent on **full-time enrollment** and satisfactory academic progress.
- **Work authorization is required** for most RA/TA funding sources; matriculation into a Caltech PhD program generally requires evidence of U.S. work authorization, absent special arrangement by the admitting option.

## 8. Funding Structure (Overview)

- **All PhD students** are offered a financial package covering **full tuition/fees plus a living stipend**, contingent on full-time enrollment and satisfactory progress (including securing an advisor).
- **Current stipend rate:** **$48,244/year (12-month)** effective September 1, 2025, rising to **$50,000/year** effective September 1, 2026. Stipends are paid monthly (by the 26th) and are taxable income.
- Funding may come from **internal/external fellowships, RAs, TAs, or a combination.**
- Common external fellowships held by Caltech graduate students include the **NSF Graduate Research Fellowship, DoD fellowships, NIH fellowships, Ford Foundation, and the Fannie and John Hertz Foundation** fellowship.
- Work-hour limits: total assistantship commitment generally **may not exceed 20 hours/week** during the academic year (up to 40 hours/week in summer); aggregate Institute-related activity beyond **62 hours/week** requires special petition to the Dean of Graduate Studies.
- Students receive **two weeks of annual vacation** plus Institute holidays, to be prearranged with their advisor.
- Outside part-time employment (up to 8 hours/week) requires prior written approval from the Dean of Graduate Studies and must not create a conflict of interest.
