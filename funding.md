# Caltech — Funding

`universities/caltech/funding.md`

> Caltech funding operates on **two entirely different models** depending on level: undergraduate aid is **100% need-based** (no merit scholarships), while PhD funding is a **near-universal guaranteed package** (tuition + stipend) tied to research/teaching service. This file separates the two accordingly.

---

## 1. Undergraduate Scholarships and Grants

- **Caltech does not have a merit-aid program.** All Caltech Scholarships and grants are **need-based**.
- Caltech commits to meeting **100% of demonstrated financial need** through a combination of grants, scholarships, work-study, and (limited) loans.
- **Grants** are "gift aid" — they do not need to be repaid or earned, and make up the majority of most students' aid packages.
- **Caltech Scholarships**: institutional/endowed gift funds; award amount is entirely determined by demonstrated need (no fixed amount).
- **Named/Endowed Scholarships**: funded by specific donor gifts; also need-based but some carry a **merit component** as an additional eligibility layer. Students are automatically considered if they meet need + specification criteria; a named scholarship typically **replaces** a portion of the student's general Caltech Scholarship (not additive on top of it). Recipients may be asked to write a thank-you letter to the donor.
- **For the Fall 2024 incoming class:** average grant aid was **just under $73,000**.
- **Average loan indebtedness for the Class of 2023:** approximately **$16,000**.
- More than **50% of Caltech undergraduates** receive need-based financial assistance.
- Caltech states its long-term aspiration is to build a permanent endowment sufficient to **eliminate institutional student loans entirely**.
- Families with income below roughly **$200,000** (with typical assets) can often expect aid covering the bulk of tuition, though this depends on the full need-analysis formula (income, assets excluding retirement/primary residence, family size, certain expenses).

## 3. Work-Study

- **Federal Work-Study** and **Caltech Work-Study**: an allotment of money a student earns through on-campus (or eligible off-campus) employment during the academic year, as part of the aid package.

## 4. Undergraduate Application Requirements for Aid

- FAFSA (federal) and CSS Profile are generally required.
- Full-time enrollment (minimum 36 units/quarter) is required to maintain the awarded aid types/amounts.
- International undergraduates: Caltech is need-aware in admission but states it meets full demonstrated need for admitted international students (see `admissions.md`).

## 5. Graduate Funding — PhD

- **Virtually all PhD students** receive a funding package covering **full tuition and fees plus a living stipend**, contingent on full-time enrollment and satisfactory academic progress (see `graduate_research.md` for mechanism detail).
- **Current stipend:** **$48,244/year** (effective Sept. 1, 2025), rising to **$50,000/year** (effective Sept. 1, 2026), paid monthly.
- Funding sources include a mix of:
  - **Research Assistantships (RA / GRA)**
  - **Teaching Assistantships (TA / GTA)**
  - **Institute fellowships**
  - **External fellowships** (see below)
- Work-authorization is generally required for RA/TA-based funding; matriculation typically requires evidence of U.S. work authorization absent special option-level arrangement.

## 6. Graduate Funding — Master's

- Caltech states it **does not generally provide financial aid to terminal master's students**.
- M.S. students may be eligible for TA/RA positions where departmentally available, but funding is far less guaranteed than for PhD students.
- A limited number of terminal master's students are admitted **without financial aid**, and Caltech explicitly frames this as carrying significant personal financial responsibility.
- Outside part-time employment is **not permitted** for graduate students without prior written approval from the Dean of Graduate Studies (up to 8 hrs/week, no conflict of interest).
