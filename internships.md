# Caltech — Internships

`universities/caltech/internships.md`

> Internship access at Caltech runs through both a **centralized career office (CALE)** and **division/program-specific channels**. This file distinguishes internal (Caltech/JPL-run research internships) from external (industry) internships, since the two operate very differently.

---

## 1. Internal (Caltech-Run) Research Internships

- **SURF (Summer Undergraduate Research Fellowships)** — Caltech's flagship internal research internship; see `undergraduate_research.md` for full detail.
- **WAVE Fellows** — internal research internship targeted at students from backgrounds underrepresented in STEM PhD programs; see `undergraduate_research.md`.
- **OTTCP Summer Internship in Entrepreneurship** — a **paid** internship run by Caltech's **Office of Technology Transfer and Corporate Partnerships (OTTCP)**. Interns support the **Caltech Seed Fund** (an early-stage venture fund) and gain exposure to technology commercialization, market research, and startup operations. Past intern projects have included AI applications in tech transfer and building business models for Caltech-affiliated startups.

## 2. NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) Internships

- **SURF@JPL** — Caltech's SURF program extended to JPL research groups; open to Caltech and eligible visiting students who are U.S. citizens or permanent residents (see `undergraduate_research.md`).
- JPL separately runs its own **JPL Internship Program** (via NASA's broader internship infrastructure), open more broadly to U.S. students nationwide, not limited to Caltech — Caltech students have privileged access given JPL's management relationship with the Institute.
- JPL internships span robotics, planetary science, spacecraft engineering, Earth science, and mission operations.

## 3. Visiting/External Student Research Programs at Caltech

These bring **non-Caltech students to Caltech** for research internships (distinct from Caltech students going elsewhere):

| Program | Description |
|---|---|
| **Amgen Scholars Program** | National program increasing research opportunities for students pursuing science careers; at Caltech, visiting students conduct research in biology, chemistry, and related biotech fields under faculty mentorship, alongside seminars and workshops preparing them for PhD or MD/PhD paths. |
| **LIGO SURF** | Open to undergraduates from any U.S. or foreign institution for gravitational-wave-related research (partly NSF REU-funded). |
| **SURF Off-Campus / Exchange Programs** | Allow Caltech students to intern at partner institutions abroad (e.g., University of Iceland exchange). |

## 4. External (Industry) Internships

Managed primarily through **CALE (Career Achievement, Leadership, and Exploration)**, Caltech's central career office, which follows a **"hub-and-spoke" model**: CALE serves as the central hub (employer relations, career coaching, recruiting infrastructure) while individual academic divisions run supplementary, discipline-specific programming.

### Key facts
- CALE reports **over 200 companies and research facilities worldwide** actively recruit Caltech students, postdocs, and alumni.
- CALE hosts **career fairs, industry days, tech talks, employer office hours**, and smaller-format networking events (e.g., "dinners for eight").
- A dedicated online recruiting platform ("Next Steps Caltech" / similar) lets approved employers post jobs/internships and view student/postdoc/alumni profiles.
- **Caltech Bioscience Industrial Partners Program (CBIPP)** connects students and postdocs interested in life-sciences careers with partner companies through structured events.
- CALE also runs **virtual joint career fairs** with peer institutions (e.g., Harvey Mudd College).

### Application guidance (per CALE)
- Students are encouraged to begin interning as early as their **underclassman years**, though the most competitive positions are typically for rising seniors.
- CALE offers workshops on resumes, interviewing, and networking, plus one-on-one advising appointments.

## 5. Industrial Partnerships (Research-Level, Not Individual Internships)

Several Caltech research centers exist specifically to formalize industry collaboration and, in some cases, feed internship pipelines:
- **Resnick Sustainability Institute** — industry-facing sustainability/energy partnerships.
- **Joint Center for Artificial Photosynthesis (JCAP)** — DOE-funded, multi-institution/industry solar-fuels consortium.
- **Kavli Nanoscience Institute (KNI)** — shared cleanroom facilities used in some industry-sponsored nanotechnology projects.

*(See `research_centers.md` for full center listings; not every center runs a formal internship pipeline — coverage here reflects only those with a documented internship/industry-facing component.)*

## 6. Undergraduate vs. Graduate Distinctions

| | Undergraduate | Graduate |
|---|---|---|
| Primary internal pathway | SURF / WAVE (summer research) | Research is typically integrated into the PhD itself (assistantships), rather than a separate "internship" |
| External industry internships | Common, especially rising-senior summer | Common for PhD students in applied fields (e.g., a summer at a national lab or company between thesis milestones), arranged individually with advisor approval |
| JPL access | Via SURF@JPL | Graduate students may work at JPL through affiliated research projects or JPL-sponsored fellowships |
| Office supporting search | CALE (all levels) | CALE + division-specific career programming |
