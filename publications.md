# Caltech — Publications

`universities/caltech/publications.md`

> Caltech's publication infrastructure is centered on **CODA** (Caltech Collection of Open Digital Archives), which houses several discrete repositories, plus a formal, faculty-adopted **Open Access Policy**. All information below is sourced from the Caltech Library.

---

## 1. Publication Repositories (CODA)

**CODA — Caltech Collection of Open Digital Archives** is the umbrella technical infrastructure for Caltech's open digital repositories, run by the Caltech Library. Its main constituent repositories include:

| Repository | Content | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **CaltechAUTHORS** | Scholarly articles, books, book chapters, and conference papers by Caltech faculty and researchers | Active since **2001**; powered by the open-source **InvenioRDM** platform; updated continuously |
| **CaltechTHESIS** | PhD theses and dissertations (plus some other degree theses) | Contains **more than 6,000 selected theses**, dating back to **1921**; since **2003**, all new PhD theses are deposited by graduate students at degree conferral |
| **CaltechDATA** | Research datasets | *(Existence confirmed via Caltech Library's broader digital-archives ecosystem; full scope not independently verified in this pass — confirm at library.caltech.edu)* |

CaltechAUTHORS supports **OAI 2.0 harvesting** (base URL: `https://authors.library.caltech.edu/oai2d`), and is indexed by services such as **OAIster** and **Google Scholar**.

## 2. Institutional Repository — CaltechAUTHORS in Detail

- **Mission:** To collect, manage, preserve, and provide global access over time to the scholarly output of the Institute and its campus units.
- The Faculty Board has charged the Library with documenting the Institute's full scholarly output; there is **no restriction** on Caltech authors' choice of publication venue or publishing agreement — CaltechAUTHORS operates in accordance with individual publishers' policies.
- Because many publishers restrict what can be posted (or require embargo periods), authors are encouraged to upload their **author's accepted manuscript** version where publisher agreements allow.
- Deposits can occur via a **Simple Deposit** web form, an **Advanced Deposit** process, or by emailing files to `coda@library.caltech.edu`.

## 3. Thesis / Dissertation Archive — CaltechTHESIS in Detail

- More than **6,000 theses and dissertations** spanning **all Caltech departments**, with the earliest dating to **1921**.
- Since 2003, deposit of the final, approved PhD thesis into CaltechTHESIS is a **required step** in the degree-conferral process (see `graduate_research.md`, Dissertation Process).
- The Caltech Library has an ongoing effort to **digitize all historical PhD theses** and make them as widely available as copyright law permits.

## 4. Open Access Policy

- Caltech's **Faculty Board adopted an Open Access Policy on June 10, 2013**, effective **January 1, 2014**.
- The policy **automatically grants Caltech a non-exclusive license** to host scholarly articles and conference papers by Caltech faculty that have been accepted for formal publication, covering works authored/co-authored while the person is Caltech faculty and published on/after Jan. 1, 2014.
- This license allows faculty to legally post the **accepted manuscript version** (post-peer-review, pre-typesetting) of their work in CaltechAUTHORS, regardless of what an individual publisher agreement might otherwise restrict.
- **Waivers:** If a publisher's agreement directly conflicts with the policy (i.e., forbids any public posting), faculty can request a **waiver** via a simple online form; the emailed approval can then be shown to the publisher.
- **Timing:** Articles are not released publicly in CaltechAUTHORS until after formal journal publication; Library staff monitor publication status and will honor publisher embargo periods unless the faculty author specifically instructs otherwise.
- **Interpretation authority:** The **Office of the Provost** is responsible for interpreting the policy and resolving disputes about its application.
- The policy is explicitly **independent of any grant-funder open access mandates** (e.g., NIH Public Access Policy) — Caltech's institutional policy and funder-specific requirements (like NIH's PubMed Central deposit mandate) operate in parallel, not as substitutes for one another.
- Caltech states an institutional commitment to the **availability, longevity, and functionality** of CaltechAUTHORS "to the extent technologically feasible," including backup and mirroring.

## 5. Library Resources

- The **Caltech Library** is the central administrator of CODA, CaltechAUTHORS, CaltechTHESIS, and the Open Access Policy infrastructure.
- General correspondence regarding CaltechAUTHORS: `library@caltech.edu`.
- CaltechAUTHORS-specific support: `coda@library.caltech.edu`.
- The Library supports researchers with technical infrastructure for **technical reports and working papers** in addition to formally published articles.
