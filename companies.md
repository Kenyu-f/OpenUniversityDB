# Caltech — Companies & Innovation Ecosystem

`universities/caltech/companies.md`

> Caltech's small size relative to peer institutions (~2,500 total students and faculty) shapes its startup ecosystem: fewer total startups than MIT or Stanford in absolute terms, but a comparatively high rate of company formation per capita. Figures below mix data of different vintages (some from 2011, 2017, and recent years) — flagged accordingly.

## 1. Technology Transfer Office

- **Office of Technology Transfer and Corporate Partnerships (OTTCP)** — Caltech's central technology-transfer and industry-partnerships office, formed when the original **Office of Technology Transfer (OTT)**, founded in **1995**, merged with the corporate partnerships function (merger reported as of ~2017).
- OTT/OTTCP was built from scratch starting in 1995, explicitly modeled on the tech-transfer offices already established at **MIT and Stanford**, as part of a broader cultural shift away from an "ivory tower" research culture.
- **As of the ~2017 retrospective:** the office had helped launch **over 240 startups** since founding, of which **~25% had successful exits** (acquisition or IPO) and **~40% remained active, viable companies**; it had also granted **more than 1,000 technology licenses**, generating **$395 million in gross revenue** for Caltech cumulatively to that point. ⚠️ *These cumulative figures are from a 2017 article and will be understated for the current date — reverify current totals via OTTCP directly.*
- Historical pace: OTT/OTTCP has been reported (as of ~2011–2013 sourcing) to spin off **approximately 8 companies per year** on average, a rate the office itself has characterized as high **per capita** relative to Caltech's small size, compared to Stanford's ~12/year (much larger student body) and MIT's ~20/year (much larger student body).
- Caltech holds **more than 2,000 active U.S. patents** and has been reported to patent as many as ~140 inventions per year (2011 figure — reverify).
- **More than 100 Caltech-affiliated companies were founded in the last decade** (per Caltech's own "Local Economic Impact" summary), with the **Caltech Seed Fund** investing **more than $1.5 million per year** to support them.

## 2. Entrepreneurship Programs and Funding Vehicles

| Program | Description |
|---|---|
| **Caltech Seed Fund** | An early-stage venture fund supporting Caltech-affiliated startups; supported in part by OTTCP entrepreneurship interns (see `internships.md`). Invests $1.5M+/year. |
| **Wilson Hill Ventures partnership** | A private venture-funding partnership (effort began Feb. 2021) formed specifically to fund Caltech science-based startups, with a goal of funding **5–10 startups per year**; works alongside OTTCP to help optimize business models and build professional networks. Open to faculty, graduate/undergraduate students, and staff. |
| **OTTCP Summer Internship in Entrepreneurship** | See `internships.md` — student support for the Seed Fund and portfolio companies. |

## 4. Innovation Ecosystem — Supporting Infrastructure

- **Kavli Nanoscience Institute (KNI)** — shared cleanroom/fabrication facilities used by some startups in early-stage hardware development (see `research_centers.md`).
- **Resnick Sustainability Institute** and **Joint Center for Artificial Photosynthesis (JCAP)** — sources of deep-tech IP in the energy/sustainability space that has fed startup formation (e.g., battery and solar-fuel technologies).
- Caltech's Pasadena location situates it within the broader **Los Angeles tech ecosystem**, and OTTCP explicitly tracks "local economic impact" as an institutional priority.
