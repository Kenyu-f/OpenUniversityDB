## 5. Podcasts and Audio/Video Lecture Archives

- **TEDxCaltech (2011)** — a full-day event honoring Richard Feynman, featuring talks on topics from interactive astronomy visualization to anti-HIV drug design; archived as a downloadable/podcast series (originally distributed via iTunes U).
- Caltech has historically distributed recorded lectures and public talks through **iTunes U** and similar academic podcast platforms — coverage of current, actively maintained podcast feeds should be reverified, as iTunes U itself has been deprecated by Apple; check Caltech's current media/events pages for whatever platform has succeeded it.

## 6. Lecture Series and Open Course Materials

- **[The Feynman Lectures on Physics — Official Online Edition](https://www.feynmanlectures.caltech.edu/)** — the complete three-volume set of Richard Feynman's legendary 1960s introductory physics lectures at Caltech, presented jointly by Caltech's PMA Division and The Feynman Lectures Website, free to read online, optimized for all device sizes. Originally recorded for Caltech undergraduates and later transcribed into the definitive published edition.
  - Includes a recorded audio reading of Feynman's 1974 Caltech commencement address, **"Cargo Cult Science."**
- **Watson Lecture Series** — Caltech's long-running public lecture series in which faculty present their research to a general audience (verify current schedule/archive location on caltech.edu).
- Individual divisions (e.g., PMA, EAS) periodically post **colloquium and seminar recordings**; check each division's site for current listings.

## 7. Digital Libraries

- **[CaltechAUTHORS](https://authors.library.caltech.edu)** — open-access repository of Caltech scholarly output (see `publications.md`).
- **[CaltechTHESIS](https://thesis.library.caltech.edu)** — open archive of 6,000+ Caltech theses/dissertations dating to 1921 (see `publications.md`).
- **[Caltech Library](https://library.caltech.edu)** — central library site, including subject guides, archives, and special collections.
- **[Caltech Archives](https://archives.caltech.edu)** — historical institutional records, papers of notable faculty (e.g., Feynman, Millikan), and Institute history.

## 8. Course Materials / OpenCourseWare-Style Resources

- Caltech does not run a single centralized "OpenCourseWare" platform on the scale of MIT OCW; open materials are distributed primarily through:
  - **The Feynman Lectures on Physics** (see above) — the most prominent freely available Caltech course material.
  - Individual faculty/department pages that sometimes post lecture notes, problem sets, or recorded lectures for specific courses — coverage varies significantly by instructor and is not centrally indexed.
- ⚠️ If a centralized Caltech OCW-equivalent exists beyond the above, it was not identified in this research pass — verify directly with the Registrar or individual divisions.

## 9. Student Organizations

- Caltech's roughly **1,000 undergraduates** support a disproportionately large number of clubs and organizations relative to the school's small size, spanning academic/professional, cultural, recreational, and service categories.
- The **Associated Students of the California Institute of Technology (ASCIT)** is the undergraduate student government body and a central resource for club information/funding.
- ⚠️ A specific, current, comprehensive club directory was not captured in this research pass — the authoritative source is Caltech's student life / ASCIT pages, which should be consulted directly for an up-to-date list.
