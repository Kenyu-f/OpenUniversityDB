# Caltech — Undergraduate Research

`universities/caltech/undergraduate_research.md`

> Undergraduate research is central to the Caltech experience — the Institute states that the large majority of undergraduates engage in mentored research at some point before graduating. This file covers every major structured pathway. Stipend figures and deadlines change yearly; verify current-cycle numbers against the official [Student-Faculty Programs (SFP) site](https://sfp.caltech.edu) before relying on them.

---

## 1. Summer Undergraduate Research Fellowships (SURF)

**SURF is Caltech's flagship undergraduate research program**, run by the Student-Faculty Programs (SFP) office, and has operated since **1979**. It is explicitly modeled on the professional grant-seeking process rather than a simple summer job.

### How it works
- Students identify a potential faculty (or JPL) mentor and collaborate with them to **define and develop a research project** before applying.
- Applicants write a **formal research proposal** describing the project.
- Faculty mentors review and support proposals; a selection committee reviews and recommends awards.
- Students carry out the project **in person, full-time, over a 10-week period** during the summer — SURF participants may not take courses or hold another job concurrently, and remote projects are not permitted.
- Fellows submit **two interim reports, a research abstract, and a final technical paper**, and present their results at one of several **SURF Seminar Days** — symposia modeled on professional technical conferences.

### Eligibility
- Open to **Caltech undergraduates** and to **non-Caltech visiting students** (funding for non-Caltech participants is more limited).
- Must be a continuing undergraduate, eligible for fall-term registration.
- Minimum cumulative GPA of **2.5/4.0**.
- Must have completed at least two semesters (or three quarters) of college.
- Must be in good disciplinary standing.

### Funding
- SURF participants are treated as **Caltech student employees**; recent cycles have paid **$600/week** (≈$6,000 total for 10 weeks).
- On-campus housing is available during the fellowship; participants purchase a declining-balance dining card for Caltech/JPL dining facilities.
- Some support for research-related travel is available as needed.

### SURF Variants
| Program | Description |
|---|---|
| **SURF (on-campus)** | Standard track — research with a Caltech faculty mentor on campus. |
| **SURF@JPL** | Research with scientists/engineers at NASA's **Jet Propulsion Laboratory**, open to Caltech and visiting students who are U.S. citizens or permanent residents; includes JPL-specific enrichment activities (tours, lectures, career advising). |
| **SURF Off-Campus** | Caltech students may conduct a SURF project at another college/university with an external mentor. |
| **Exchange Programs** | E.g., a Caltech–University of Iceland exchange, giving students the chance to conduct research abroad. |
| **LIGO SURF** | Focused on gravitational-wave astronomy research with the **LIGO** project; partly funded through the NSF **Research Experiences for Undergraduates (REU)** program; open to undergraduates from any U.S. or foreign institution. |
| **KNI SURF-the-WAVE (STW) Prize** | A supplemental prize fellowship from the **Kavli Nanoscience Institute** for incoming WAVE Fellows pursuing nanoscience-related projects (quantum engineering, photonics, bio-/medical devices, etc.). |

### Application Timeline (typical)
- **Announcements of Opportunity** (project listings from JPL and Caltech mentors) are typically published **November–February**.
- Students should contact and secure a mentor **before** submitting a formal application.
- Non-Caltech applicants must submit **two letters of recommendation** (at least one from a faculty member) plus an unofficial transcript.

## 2. WAVE Fellows Program

**WAVE Fellows** is a 10-week summer research program specifically designed to increase access to Caltech's PhD programs for undergraduates from **backgrounds historically underrepresented in STEM graduate education**, who are seriously considering pursuing a STEM doctorate.

### How it works
- Applicants identify **several possible faculty mentors** they're interested in working with (rather than a single pre-arranged mentor, distinguishing it somewhat from standard SURF).
- Once accepted, Fellows work with their matched mentor to define and develop a research project, carried out over 10 weeks.
- Same reporting structure as SURF: two interim reports, a research abstract, and a final paper.
- Concludes with an oral or poster presentation at a Seminar Day.
- Includes structured **academic and professional development programming** (graduate school admissions guidance, research career panels, etc.) and a **WAVE Council** of current Caltech graduate student mentors/ambassadors.

### Eligibility
- Current **sophomores through non-graduating seniors**.
- Must be **U.S. citizens, U.S. permanent residents, or DACA recipients**.
- Minimum cumulative GPA of **3.4/4.0**.
- Competitive applicants have completed sophomore-level coursework in their target research field and can articulate fit with Caltech's research strengths; prior research experience strengthens an application.

### Funding
- Recent cycles: approximately **$6,000–$7,700** for the 10-week period (figure has varied by year), plus an on-campus housing, dining, and travel supplement (~$1,000–$2,300 depending on year).
- Application deadlines have typically fallen in **early January** (e.g., January 9 in recent cycles).

## 3. Academic-Year Independent Research

- Caltech undergraduates can pursue **research for course credit** during the academic year (fall/winter/spring terms) in addition to summer programs, typically arranged directly with a faculty member.
- Many options culminate in a **Senior/Bachelor's Thesis**, particularly common in divisions such as Geological and Planetary Sciences, where a B.S. thesis is a capstone research requirement. Thesis-linked prizes exist at the department level (see Awards below).
- Research conducted during the year is often a continuation of, or preparation for, a SURF or WAVE summer project.

---

## 4. Research Assistant (RA) Positions

- Outside the structured SURF/WAVE cycles, undergraduates may be hired directly by faculty labs as **part-time research assistants**, paid hourly, during the academic year or summer.
- These positions are arranged informally between student and faculty mentor (often after a successful SURF placement) rather than through a centralized application system.

---

## 5. Undergraduate Publication and Presentation Opportunities

- **SURF Seminar Day / WAVE Seminar Day**: end-of-summer oral and poster presentation symposia, structured like professional academic conferences, open to the Caltech community.
- Selection criteria for SURF explicitly favor **projects with potential for eventual publication in refereed journals**, meaning strong SURF projects are frequently developed into co-authored papers with faculty mentors.
- Undergraduates who complete a B.S. thesis may have their work archived through Caltech's institutional repository (see `publications.md`).

---

## 6. Research Awards and Prizes

- **KNI SURF-the-WAVE (STW) Prize** — supplemental award for WAVE Fellows conducting nanoscience-related research (see above).
- Department-level senior thesis prizes exist across divisions (e.g., best B.S. thesis awards in Geological and Planetary Sciences); coverage of every departmental prize should be verified division-by-division against official department pages.
- SURF and WAVE selection itself functions as a competitive award, given the proposal-review process.

## 7. Application Process Summary

| Step | SURF | WAVE Fellows |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Identify and contact a potential mentor | Identify several possible mentors |
| 2 | Collaborate with mentor to define a project | Get matched with a mentor after acceptance |
| 3 | Write and submit a research proposal | Submit online application |
| 4 | Faculty review and award recommendation | Selection committee review |
| 5 | 10-week full-time, in-person research | 10-week full-time, in-person research |
| 6 | Interim reports, abstract, final paper | Interim reports, abstract, final paper |
| 7 | SURF Seminar Day presentation | Seminar Day presentation |
