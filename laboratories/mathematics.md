> Part of the `laboratories/` series (see [`README.md`](README.md) for the full division index). Covers the **Mathematics option**, housed within the **Division of Physics, Mathematics and Astronomy (PMA)**.

## ⚠️ A note on "laboratories" in pure mathematics

Unlike experimental sciences, Caltech's Mathematics option does not organize research into PI-directed physical "laboratories" with equipment and lab space. Instead, research is organized into **thematic research groups**, each associated with a cluster of affiliated faculty who share overlapping interests (e.g., algebra, analysis, topology). The table below uses this research-group structure in place of a traditional lab listing, consistent with how the department itself presents its research on the official site. There is no single "director" per group in the way an experimental lab has a PI — groups are collegial and multi-faculty, so the "Director" column is left blank or marked "Collegial group (no single PI)" where appropriate.

## Cross-listing note

Several Caltech mathematicians hold joint or affiliated appointments relevant to **Computing and Mathematical Sciences (CMS)** and **Applied and Computational Mathematics (ACM)** — these appear as separate options/programs in Caltech's catalog and will be covered in their own laboratories files (`laboratories/computing-and-mathematical-sciences.md`, if warranted) to avoid duplicating faculty listings across files. Where a research group above overlaps with CMS/ACM, this is noted.
