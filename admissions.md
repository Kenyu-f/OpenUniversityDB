# Caltech — Admissions

`universities/caltech/admissions.md`

> Covers undergraduate and graduate admissions processes, requirements, deadlines, and funding for admitted students. Requirements, deadlines, and testing policy change annually — **always verify against the official pages linked in Section 6 before relying on any date or numeric threshold below.**

---

## 1. Undergraduate Admissions

Caltech undergraduate admission is **need-blind for U.S. citizens and permanent residents** and **highly selective**, with an acceptance rate around **3%**, among the lowest of any U.S. university. Admission is holistic; Caltech does not publish formal minimum GPA or test-score cutoffs.

### Application Rounds

| Round | Deadline (typical) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Restrictive Early Action (REA) | November 1 | Non-binding but restrictive — applicants generally may not apply Early Action/Early Decision elsewhere (with limited exceptions, e.g., public university non-binding rolling programs). Supplemental materials due by Nov. 6; testing completed by Nov. 30. Decisions released mid-December. |
| Regular Decision | January 5 | Standard round. Decisions typically in March. |
| Reply deadline | May 1 | Common Reply Date for admitted students to confirm enrollment. |

### Application Requirements (First-Year/Undergraduate)

- **Application platform:** Common Application (Caltech-specific supplement included).
- **Standardized testing:** As of Fall 2025 entry, Caltech **requires** submission of either the SAT or the ACT (ACT Writing section not required). This reverses Caltech's earlier test-blind policy adopted during 2021–2024; **confirm current-year policy before citing**, as testing requirements have changed multiple times in recent admissions cycles.
- **Academic preparation requirements:** Calculus, chemistry, and physics coursework (or approved substitutes). For applicants without access to these courses, Caltech accepts certification or "course challenge" completion through **Schoolhouse.world** in AP/College Calculus BC, AP/College Chemistry, or AP/College Physics 1, in lieu of formal coursework.
- **Teacher evaluations / letters of recommendation:** Required (typically from math/science teachers per Caltech's supplement instructions).
- **School report and transcript:** Submitted by school counselor, generally through the Common App.
- **Supplemental essays:** Caltech-specific short-answer and essay prompts (updated annually).
- **English Proficiency Exam:** Required of international applicants unless English is their native language or the primary language of instruction at their secondary school. Accepted exams: **Duolingo English Test (DET), IELTS, or TOEFL.** Must be completed before the application deadline.
- **Application fee or fee waiver.**

### International Applicants (Undergraduate)

- International applicants follow the same academic review process as domestic applicants.
- Caltech is **need-aware for international applicants** (unlike its need-blind policy for U.S. citizens/permanent residents), because financial aid funds for international students are limited — though Caltech states it meets full demonstrated need for admitted international students.
- An **International Financial Aid Statement of Intent** is required during the application to confirm understanding of aid policy.
- No separate international application deadline; same REA/Regular Decision structure applies.

### GPA Expectations

Caltech does not publish a formal minimum GPA. Class profile data (self-reported by Caltech and secondary sources) indicates the vast majority of admitted students rank at or near the top of their high school class, with the overwhelming majority in the top decile.

## 2. Graduate Admissions

Graduate admission at Caltech is **decentralized by department/option** — each of the roughly 30+ graduate options (within the six divisions) runs its own admissions committee and sets its own supplementary requirements, though all are coordinated through the central **Graduate Studies Office**.

### General Timeline

| Stage | Typical timing |
|---|---|
| Application window opens | ~November 21 |
| Department deadlines | Vary by option, generally **December 1 – December 15** (a few as early as Nov. 22) |
| Decisions released | By approximately **April 1** (varies by department) |
| Reply / acceptance deadline | **April 15** (aligned with the CGS April 15 Resolution common across U.S. graduate programs) |

Applicants should always check the specific department's admissions page, since deadlines differ by a few weeks across options (e.g., Biology: Nov. 22; Bioengineering: Dec. 1; Chemistry and Chemical Engineering: variable).

### Application Requirements (Graduate, general across departments)

- Bachelor's degree (or equivalent) completed before matriculation. Applicants who already hold a PhD are generally **not** considered for a second PhD at Caltech.
- **Transcripts** from every college/university attended.
- **Three letters of recommendation** (the application system accepts a maximum of three).
- **CV / resume.**
- **Statement of Purpose** (length varies by department; e.g., Geological and Planetary Sciences specifies ~2 pages), describing research interests, background, and fit with Caltech faculty.
- **GRE:** Policy **varies by department** and has shifted significantly institution-wide toward optional/not-required in recent cycles:
  - Chemistry and Chemical Engineering: **not required**; submission neither helps nor hurts if omitted.
  - Mechanical and Civil Engineering: **optional**; self-reported scores considered if submitted.
  - Geological and Planetary Sciences: **required** for geology, geochemistry, geobiology, and geophysics tracks; recommended but not required for environmental science/engineering and planetary science tracks.
  - Electrical Engineering: encouraged but policy should be checked directly with the department.
  - **Always verify current GRE policy directly with the specific graduate option**, as Caltech states it is reviewing GRE use institution-wide.
- **English Proficiency Exam** (TOEFL/IELTS/DET) for international applicants whose native language is not English or who were not instructed in English.
- **No minimum GPA** is formally required at the institute level; GPA is one holistic factor among many, and applicants are invited to contextualize any GPA anomalies within the Statement of Purpose.

### Interviews

Interviews are **not uniformly required** across all graduate programs; some departments conduct optional or invitation-based interviews (particularly for competitive PhD programs or specific fellowships), but this is determined at the department level rather than by a university-wide policy. Applicants should check with their specific target option.

### Advisor / Lab Matching

Graduate admission at Caltech, particularly for PhD programs, places significant weight on **fit between the applicant's research interests and specific Caltech faculty members' active research programs** — a feature more pronounced here than at some peer institutions. This is elaborated further in `graduate_research.md`.

## 4. Funding for Admitted Students

### Undergraduate
- Caltech is **need-blind for U.S. citizens/permanent residents** and commits to meeting **100% of demonstrated financial need** through grants and work-study (not loans, per Caltech's no-loan policy for need-based aid).
- More than 50% of undergraduates receive need-based financial aid; Caltech states that over 75% of students graduate debt-free.
- International undergraduates: need-aware in admission, but Caltech states it meets full demonstrated need for admitted international students once admitted.

### Graduate
- On average, **more than 98% of admitted graduate students** receive a funding package covering full tuition plus a stipend.
- Funding sources include **research assistantships (RAs), teaching assistantships (TAs), Institute fellowships, and external fellowships** (e.g., NSF Graduate Research Fellowship).
- Terminal master's students are a notable exception and are frequently self-funded or sponsor-funded.
- PhD matriculation generally requires evidence of U.S. work authorization, since most funding mechanisms require it (special arrangements are sometimes possible by individual admitting options).
- Full detail in `funding.md`.
